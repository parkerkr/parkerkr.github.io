Priorities as a Teacher

My teaching approach focuses on ways of best preparing students for the workforce, sharing my love of learning, and to making a difference in the lives of my students. My courses are designed to develop and hone students’ critical and analytical thinking skills. Students are required to reason out precise solutions to unstructured problems and to clearly express those solutions in a form that computers (or developers) can understand. Experiential assignments are designed to reinforce course concepts and to simulate enterprise situations so that students obtain meaningful hands-on experience that also serves as a vehicle for demonstrating and practicing basic principles.

I enjoy interacting with students, and one of my strengths as a teacher has always been my ability to develop a strong rapport with students. Students quickly realize that I genuinely care about them, not just as students but as individuals, and they often seem to respond by working harder. I use humor during lectures to keep students’ attention, and I tend to be quite animated in the classroom, which helps me keep students’ focus. I am enthusiastic about teaching and students respond to this. One student evaluation comment noted, “His excitement for the subject matter helped me to really like it.” I encourage students to ask questions in class, during office hours, and via email. For me, this one-on-one interaction with my students is essential for their learning and for my enjoyment of teaching.

I want to share with students my love of learning and to make a difference in their lives. I find it fulfilling when former students tell me that they are making an immediate impression on their employers, and they attribute it to my teaching. To know that you have had a positive impact on even one individual’s life makes all the hard work and preparation worth the effort.
